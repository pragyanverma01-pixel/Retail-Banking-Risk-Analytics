Retail Banking Portfolio & Risk Analytics
Business Objective
Designed and deployed an end-to-end relational database simulating a regional Indian retail banking portfolio (Tier 2/3 cities). The objective was to analyze credit utilization risk, identify high-value consumer segments, and evaluate the market penetration of premium credit products.

Methodology & Tools
Data Architecture (SQL): Engineered a 4-table normalized relational database (Customers, Accounts, CardProducts, Transactions) using primary/foreign key constraints.

Data Generation (Python/SQL): Generated a mathematically distributed, hyper-realistic dataset of 50 users and 100+ transactions, simulating organic financial behaviors (e.g., fractional cents, non-linear credit utilization, regional demographics).

Business Intelligence (Power BI): Executed ETL processes to flatten the SQL architecture into a master view, developing an interactive dashboard to visualize key risk metrics.

Key Insights
Risk Identification: Utilized scatter plot modeling to isolate high-risk accounts (low credit score + >80% credit utilization) for targeted intervention.

Revenue Drivers: Identified 'Electronics' and 'Travel' as the highest-grossing merchant categories by transaction volume, providing data to optimize future cashback reward strategies.

Product Adoption: Visualized the distribution of Elite Metal vs. Basic Savings accounts to track premium product market share.
